## Dimitrios S. Nikolopoulos

Dimitrios Nikolopoulos is the John W. Hancock Professor of Engineering, Professor in the Department of Computer Science, and Professor by courtesy in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Virginia Tech. He conducts research in Computer Systems and High-Performance Computing. Some of his prior research has been adopted in the [OpenMP parallel programming standard](https://www.openmp.org), [RedHat Enterprise Linux](https://www.redhat.com/en/technologies/linux-platforms/enterprise-linux),  several commercial system software products including parallelizing compilers, in-memory databases, and data stream processing systems, as well as numerous experimental operating systems and programming languages for multicore and multithreaded servers. More details about his research are available in his [DBLP](https://dblp.org/pid/50/1235.html), [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=PkCuYUQAAAAJ&hl=en), [ORCID](https://orcid.org/0000-0003-0217-8307), or [Scopus](https://www.scopus.com/authid/detail.uri?authorId=7004096576) profiles. Dimitrios is teaching courses in [Computer Organization](https://cs.vt.edu/Undergraduate/courses/CS2506.html), [Systems and Networking Capstone](https://cs.vt.edu/Undergraduate/courses/CS4284.html), [Multiprocessor Programming](https://cs.vt.edu/Graduate/Courses/GradCourseDescriptions.html#CS510) and [Warehouse-Scale Computing](https://cs.vt.edu/Graduate/Courses/GradCourseDescriptions.html#CS5914). 

Dimitrios is an [IEEE Fellow](https://www.ieee.org/membership/fellows/index.html), a [Royal Society Wolfson Fellow](https://royalsociety.org/grants-schemes-awards/grants/royal-society-wolfson-fellowship/), a [Fellow of the BCS](https://www.bcs.org/membership-and-registrations/become-a-member/bcs-fellowship/), a [Fellow of the IET](https://www.theiet.org/membership/become-a-member/fellow-membership/), and a [Distinguished Member of the ACM](https://awards.acm.org/distinguished-members). In addition, he has received numerous prestigious personal investigator and faculty awards (including the NSF CAREER, DOE Early Career, IBM Faculty Award, Cisco Faculty Awards, SFI-DEL Investigator Award, Marie Curie Individual Fellowship), nine best paper awards from premier conferences (including SC, PPoPP, IPDPS, CCGRID, and DATE), three Best Paper Award nominations (from ICS, SC, and HPDC) and two awards for editorial excellence (from the [IEEE Transactions on Parallel and Distributed Systems](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/xpl/RecentIssue.jsp?punumber=71) and [Sustainable Computing: Informatics and Systems](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/sustainable-computing-informatics-and-systems)). He has chaired the Program Committee of the [ACM International Conference on Supercomputing](https://www.ics-conference.org/) in 2022 and 2023. His research has been supported with $36.5 million of external funding (personal share of a total of $103.7 million in competitive funding) from NSF, DOE, the European Commission, EPSRC, SFI, IBM, Cisco, and Sony. 

Dimitrios served in major University leadership roles, including Head of the School of [EEECS at QUB](https://www.qub.ac.uk/schools/eeecs/), Director of the [ECIT Global Innovation Institute](https://www.google.com/search?client=safari&rls=en&q=ECIT+QUB&ie=UTF-8&oe=UTF-8&safe=active), and Director of the [QUB HPC Working Group](https://www.qub.ac.uk/directorates/InformationServices/Services/HighPerformanceComputing/). During his tenure in leadership roles, he received on behalf of the School two awards for efforts to improve diversity and inclusion ([Athena Swan Silver](https://www.advance-he.ac.uk/equality-charters/athena-swan-charter), [Investors in People Silver](https://www.investorsinpeople.com)). 

Dimitrios currently leads the [PEARL (Performance and Resiliency)](https://pearl-lab.gitlab.io) lab at Virginia Tech. He is also Associate Director of the [Stack@CS Center for Computer Systems](https://www.google.com/search?client=safari&rls=en&q=Stack%40CS+VT&ie=UTF-8&oe=UTF-8&safe=active), with a remit to advance cross-stack collaborative research against significant challenges in computing systems. 

Dimitrios is actively looking for motivated Ph.D. students to join PEARL. Students interested in high-performance computing, computer systems, and any cross-cutting system challenges arising from the interaction between emerging applications and hardware are welcome to apply. Interested
students should send a brief description of their interests and any research experience (e.g., projects, publications) or relevant technical experience (e.g., software or hardware artifacts) that they have.

PEARL also welcomes MSc (with thesis option), Undergraduate, and High School students for term internships.
