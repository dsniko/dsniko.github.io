Dimitrios S. Nikolopoulos is the John W. Hancock Professor of Engineering at Virginia Tech, where he conducts research in high-performance computing and system software. His work has influenced industry standards such as OpenMP and impacted many system software products, including parallelizing compilers and in-memory databases. He has secured over $36.5 million in research funding and mentored numerous Ph.D. students and postdoctoral researchers. Recognized as an IEEE Fellow, an ACM Distinguished Member, and a Fellow of several international computing, engineering, and artificial intelligence societies, he has received prestigious awards, including the NSF CAREER Award, the DOE Early Career PI Award, the Royal Society Wolfson Research Merit Award, faculty awards from IBM, Cisco, and Sony, and multiple Best Paper Awards at top-tier conferences.
